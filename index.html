The Battle For Castle Itter

The Battle For Castle Itter was one of the strangest battles ever to be had during the Second World War. Pitting both American and defected German troops against German SS, both sides fought over French POW's. One side aiming to capture and kill them, while the other saught to protect them, the final battle of WWII took place.

Background

The Battle for Castle Itter was one of the last battles to be fought in the European Theater during World War 2. Fought on May 5th, 1945 in the Austrian North Tyrol Castle Itter. The castle is a small one on the hill just north of the village of Itter in Austria, occupied by the German army during the war. General Oswald Pohl seized the castle from the previous owner, Franz Grüner on February 7th, 1943. Pohl then transformed the castle into a prison camp, completing its transformation on the 25th of April 1943. The prison was established to contain high-profile French prisoners that were deemed valuable to the Reich. Besides these "VIP Prisoners", the castle held numerous Eastern European prisoners detached from the Dachau Concentration Camp who were used for maintenance around the castle.

The Battle

A very good synopsis of the battle in the form of a video if you would rather listen instead of read.

On May 3rd 1945, the imprisoned Yugoslav communist resistance member Zvonimir Čučković working as a handyman at the prison, left the castle for preform errands for the prisons commander Sebastian Wimmer Čučković carried a letter written in English, asking for Allied aid to, to give to the first American he found.

Čučković found his way to Innsbruck and reached the advanced party of the 409th Infantry Regiment of the American 103rd Infantry Division and informed them of the prisoners residing in the castle. They were not permited to mount a rescue mission at the moment, but ensured an answer from HQ in the morning. At dawn, a heavily armored rescue mission begun but was swiftly stopped by shelling just about half way to Itter.

On May 2nd, the prisons commander, Sebastian Wimmer, feared for his life after the former commanders death and Čučković's failure to return. Shortly after the SS-Totenkopfverbände guards followed suite and left the prisoners there who then armed themselves and took over the castle for themselves.

The prison leaders, not knowing the results of Čučković's effort, sent Andreas Krobot to the city of Wörgl with a similar note. He then succeeded in contacting Austrian resistance in that town which had recently been abandoned by Wehrmacht forces but was reoccupied by Waffen-SS troops. Andreas was then taken to Major Josef Gangl, who sought to maintain his presence in town to protect the locals from the SS. Gangl's hopes where for the Americans to reach the town and surrender to them, but now he must ask for there help.

At the same time this was happening, a recon team of four sherman tanks commanded by Captain Lee was asked to provide relief by Gangl, without hesitation, Lee volunteered to lead a rescue mission.

After personal reconnaissance of the castle with Gangl himself, Lee began his assault on the castle. Leaving one of his tanks at the bridge to guard it, he set off accompanies only by 14 American soldiers, Gangl, a driver for Gangl's vehicle, and a truck carrying ten former German artillerymen. 4 miles from the castle, they defeated a party of SS troops that had been attempting to set up a roadblock.

Meanwhile, the French Prisoners had requested an SS officer, Kurt- Siegfried Schrader, whom they befriended in Itter, to take charge of their defense. Once Lee arrived at the castle, prisoners welcomed them and warmly greeted them, but were dissapointed in the small size of the rescue force. defying Lee's orders, the French prisoners remained outside and fought alongside the American and Wehrmacht soldiers. On the night of May 4th, the defenders were harassed by reconnaissance forces sent to gauge their strength and weaknesses.

On the morning of May 5th, 1945, Only 5 days after Hitlers Suicide, a 150 strong force of Waffen-SS mounted their attack. Before the main assault, Gangl phoned Alois Mayr, the Austrian resistance leade in Wörgl and request reinforcements. Only 2 German soldiers and a teen, Hans Waltl, from the resistance could be spared. The Sherman tank was able to support via machine gun fire, until it was destroyed by German 88 fire. In the meantime, come afternoon, the 142nd Infantry Regiment of the despiration of the defenders, and dispatched a relief force. Aware of his flaw in not giving the 142nd complete information, Lee accepted tennis star Jean Borotra's offer to vault the castle wall and run the gauntlet of SS ambushes to deliver it. He succeeded, requested a uniform, then joined the reinforcements as they made their way to the castle with haste before the defenders where no more.

The reinforcements arrived around 4 pm, and the SS were swiftly defeated. Among the defending party's dead, Josef Gangl. He died trying to relocate the French Prime Minister where he would not be harmed. Upwards of 100 prisoners were reportedly taken, and the French Prisoners were released and and made it to Paris by May 10th.
